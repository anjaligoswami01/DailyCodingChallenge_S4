Data modeling interviews evaluate a candidate's ability to design scalable and efficient database structures.

Interviewers commonly ask about normalization and denormalization. Candidates should understand how normalization reduces redundancy while denormalization improves analytical performance.

Dimensional modeling is a key topic for data warehouse roles. Engineers should be familiar with fact tables, dimension tables, star schemas, and snowflake schemas.

A common interview exercise involves designing a schema for an e-commerce, banking, or inventory management system. Candidates must identify entities, relationships, and business requirements.

Slowly Changing Dimensions (SCDs) frequently appear in warehouse-focused interviews. Understanding Type 1, Type 2, and Type 3 SCD implementations is important.

Granularity is another important concept. Engineers should understand how the level of detail stored in fact tables affects reporting flexibility.

Performance considerations such as indexing, partitioning, and query optimization may also be discussed.

Successful candidates demonstrate both technical understanding and the ability to translate business requirements into scalable data models.

Strong data modeling skills help organizations build reliable reporting systems and maintain analytical consistency.
