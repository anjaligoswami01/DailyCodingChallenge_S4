Apache Spark is a common topic in data engineering interviews due to its widespread use in large-scale data processing.

Interviewers often begin with Spark architecture. Candidates should understand drivers, executors, clusters, tasks, stages, and jobs. Explaining how Spark distributes computations demonstrates a strong conceptual foundation.

RDDs, DataFrames, and Datasets are frequently discussed. DataFrames are generally preferred because they support schema awareness and benefit from Spark's Catalyst Optimizer.

Transformations and actions are key concepts. Candidates should understand lazy evaluation and how Spark builds execution plans before performing computations.

Performance tuning is another important area. Interviewers may ask about partitioning, caching, broadcast joins, shuffle operations, and file formats such as Parquet.

Questions about Spark SQL are common because many organizations use SQL-style processing on distributed datasets. Engineers should understand DataFrame operations and SQL integration.

Structured Streaming topics may appear for real-time processing roles. Concepts such as checkpointing, watermarking, and fault tolerance are important.

Hands-on project experience is often more valuable than theoretical knowledge. Candidates who have built Spark pipelines can explain practical optimization strategies and troubleshooting techniques.

Strong Spark knowledge demonstrates readiness for large-scale data engineering environments.
