A star schema is a widely used data modeling technique in data warehousing. It organizes data into fact tables and dimension tables to support efficient analytical querying.

The fact table contains measurable business events such as sales transactions, orders, website visits, or inventory movements. Facts typically include numeric metrics and foreign keys referencing dimensions.

Dimension tables provide descriptive context. Examples include customer, product, location, and time dimensions. These tables contain attributes used for filtering, grouping, and reporting.

The schema resembles a star because dimension tables surround the central fact table. This structure simplifies query design and improves analytical performance.

For example, a sales fact table may contain transaction amounts linked to customer, product, and date dimensions. Analysts can easily calculate revenue by region, product category, or customer segment.

Star schemas reduce the number of joins required for reporting compared to highly normalized transactional databases. This makes them ideal for business intelligence workloads.

Modern cloud data warehouses frequently use star schemas because they align well with SQL-based analytics and dashboarding tools.

Data engineers often design star schemas during warehouse development projects to improve query performance and create intuitive analytical datasets.

Understanding star schemas is essential for building scalable reporting environments and supporting enterprise decision-making processes.
