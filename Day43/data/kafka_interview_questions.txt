Apache Kafka is widely used for real-time data streaming and event-driven architectures. As a result, Kafka-related questions frequently appear in data engineering interviews.

Candidates should understand core concepts including topics, partitions, producers, consumers, brokers, and consumer groups. These components form the foundation of Kafka architecture.

Partitioning is a common interview topic because it affects scalability and ordering. Engineers should explain how partition keys determine message placement.

Consumer groups allow multiple consumers to process data in parallel. Interviewers often ask how Kafka distributes partitions among consumers and handles failures.

Offset management is another important concept. Candidates should understand how consumers track progress and recover from interruptions.

Questions about fault tolerance typically focus on replication, leader election, and broker recovery. Understanding these mechanisms demonstrates knowledge of production environments.

Performance discussions may include throughput optimization, batching, compression, retention policies, and partition sizing.

Interviewers may also ask about Kafka Connect and Schema Registry. These tools simplify integration and schema management in enterprise systems.

Practical examples involving streaming analytics, fraud detection, or real-time dashboards help demonstrate applied Kafka expertise.
