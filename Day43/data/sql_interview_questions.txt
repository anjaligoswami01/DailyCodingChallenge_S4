SQL is one of the most frequently tested skills in data engineering interviews. Employers use SQL questions to evaluate a candidate's ability to retrieve, transform, and analyze data efficiently.

Common interview topics include joins, aggregations, subqueries, common table expressions (CTEs), window functions, indexing, and query optimization. Candidates should be comfortable working with multiple tables and understanding relationships between datasets.

A frequently asked question is finding the second-highest salary from an employee table. Solutions may involve subqueries, ranking functions, or dense ranking techniques. Another common problem involves identifying duplicate records using GROUP BY and HAVING clauses.

Window functions such as ROW_NUMBER(), RANK(), DENSE_RANK(), LEAD(), and LAG() are increasingly important in modern interviews. Interviewers often test ranking, running totals, and trend analysis scenarios.

Aggregation questions are also common. Candidates may be asked to calculate monthly sales, top-performing customers, or average transaction values. Understanding GROUP BY and HAVING is essential.

Performance optimization topics include indexing, partitioning, execution plans, and reducing full table scans. Data engineers should understand how databases execute queries and how optimization improves efficiency.

Preparation involves solving SQL problems regularly and practicing with real-world datasets. Platforms such as LeetCode and HackerRank provide valuable exercises.

Strong SQL skills remain one of the most important qualifications for data engineering roles.
